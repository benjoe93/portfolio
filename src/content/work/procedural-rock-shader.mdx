## The problem

Tiled rock materials read as obviously tiled the moment the camera pulls back.
The goal was a material that stays convincing from macro to micro without a
unique high-res texture per rock.

## Approach

- **Triplanar projection** to kill UV stretching on steep faces.
- A **runtime erosion mask** driven by world-space curvature so wear collects in
  crevices and peaks catch highlights.
- A small **baking tool** (in the repo) that precomputes curvature so the runtime
  cost stays low.

```hlsl
// Curvature-weighted erosion blend (simplified)
float curv = saturate(dot(ddx(worldNormal), ddx(worldPos)) * curvGain);
float erosion = smoothstep(erodeLow, erodeHigh, curv * mask);
albedo = lerp(baseRock, erodedRock, erosion);
```

## Result

One material, dozens of reused rock meshes, no visible tiling — and the erosion
responds to mesh shape for free.
